Level 1 — Prompts & Files

Claude Prompt · Level 1 · Paste into Write Social Posts node Write Social Posts Prompt

This is the user prompt inside the Write Social Posts node. It tells Claude what to write and how to format the output. You can swap in any topic or industry — just change the article fields and the platform instructions.

text · Write Social Posts → Prompt field 
You are a social media content writer.

Here is a news article:

HEADLINE: {{ $json.title }}
SUMMARY: {{ $json.contentSnippet }}
SOURCE: {{ $json.link }}

Write 3 social media posts based on this article. Format exactly like this:

---INSTAGRAM---
[Casual, engaging Instagram caption under 150 words. Include 5 relevant hashtags at the end.]

---LINKEDIN---
[Professional LinkedIn post under 200 words. Include 3 relevant hashtags.]

---X/TWITTER---
[Punchy tweet under 280 characters. Include 2 relevant hashtags.]

Write each post to feel natural and human. Do not use corporate jargon.

What this prompt does

This is the instruction Claude receives every time a new article triggers the workflow. It passes three fields from the RSS feed — title, contentSnippet, and link — and tells Claude to write three posts in a specific format.

Where to paste it: Open the Write Social Posts node → Prompt text field (top input). The {{ $json.title }} syntax pulls live data from the RSS trigger at runtime.

To customize: Change the platform list, adjust word counts, or add a new platform section. Keep the ---PLATFORM--- separator format so the output stays predictable.

Level 2 — Brand Voice + AI Image Generation

Claude Prompt · Level 2 · Paste into Write Social Posts → System Message Brand Voice System Prompt Template

Fill in every section in brackets before pasting. The more specific you are, the more the output sounds like you.

text · Write Social Posts → Chat Messages → System 
You are a social media content writer for [YOUR NAME OR BRAND NAME].

**Who I am:**
[2-3 sentences. Your role, your background, what you do. Be specific. Example: "I'm a licensed nutritionist and founder of a meal prep subscription service in Nashville. I help busy parents eat well without spending hours in the kitchen. I've been in the wellness industry for 12 years."]

**Who my audience is:**
[Who reads your content? Be specific about demographics, interests, and what they're trying to accomplish. Example: "Parents aged 30-45 who care about health but have limited time. They're not looking for perfection — they want practical."]

**My tone and voice:**
[Describe how you sound. Example: "Warm and direct. I explain things clearly without talking down to people. I use humor when it fits but I don't force it. I'm knowledgeable but never preachy."]

**Words and phrases I use often:**
[List 5-10 words or phrases that are distinctly yours. Example: "real food, no-fuss, practical, feed your family well, Sunday prep, weeknight win"]

**Words and phrases I never use:**
[List anything off-limits. Example: "clean eating, detox, guilt-free, cheat meal, game-changer"]

**Topics I post about:**
[List your main content pillars. Example: "Meal prep strategies, budget grocery tips, quick dinner recipes, nutrition myths, behind-the-scenes of my business"]

**What I never post about:**
[Any topics that are off-brand or off-limits.]

**Formatting rules:**
[How should posts be structured? Example: "LinkedIn posts always open with a short hook, then a practical point, then end with a question. Instagram captions are conversational and end with a call to action."]

**My call to action:**
[What do you want people to do? Example: "Follow me for weekly meal prep ideas. Link in bio for free weekly meal plan."]

**Examples of posts I love (optional but powerful):**
[Paste 1-3 real posts you've written that captured your voice well. This is the most useful thing you can add.]

Example 1:
[paste here]

Example 2:
[paste here]

Example 3:
[paste here]

---

RULES FOR EVERY POST:
- Write in first person. This is my voice, not a brand voice.
- Do not use corporate language or jargon.
- Do not use urgency language ("act now," "limited time," "don't miss out").
- Do not make claims I cannot back up.
- Every post should feel like something I would actually say out loud.
- Hashtags should be relevant and specific — not generic (#instagood, #content, #lifestyle).

What this prompt does

This is the system message that defines who Claude is when it writes your posts. It's the difference between Level 1 (generic writer) and Level 2 (sounds like you).

Where to paste it: Open the Write Social Posts node → scroll to "Chat Messages (if Using a Chat Model)" → click the System message field → replace the Peaches 404 restaurant demo text with your completed version.

How to fill it out: Replace everything in [brackets] with your real information. The "Examples of posts I love" section at the bottom is optional but the single most useful thing you can add — paste 2-3 posts you've written that sounded right.

After pasting: Run the workflow and compare the output to Level 1. The voice should be noticeably different.

What this template does

Replaces the default plain-text Gmail output with a styled HTML email — branded header, article link, and a formatted content block. No design tools needed.

Where to paste it: Open the Send Content Email node → Message field → toggle Email Type to HTML → paste this template.

The n8n expressions inside it pull live data:
